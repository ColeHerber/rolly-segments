================================================================================
  ESP32-S3 CAN + IMU BOARD — FULL CIRCUIT DIAGRAM  (Rev B)
  MCU:   ESP32-S3-WROOM-1-N8
  IMU:   ISM6HG256X (SPI)
  CAN:   SN65HVD230 + 2× JST GH 4-circuit (DroneCAN dual-port daisy-chain)

================================================================================

  USB-C Pin  GND  ──────────────────────────────────────────── GND

  USB-C Pin  CC2  ──[R8: 5.1kΩ]──► GND   ← device-mode pull-down
  USB-C Pin  D–   ──[U6: USBLC6-2P6 ESD]──► ESP32 IO19 (USB D–)
  USB-C Pin  D+   ──[U6: USBLC6-2P6 ESD]──► ESP32 IO20 (USB D+)
  USB-C Shield ──────────────────────────────────────────────── GND

  USBLC6-2P6 (U6) connections  [SOT23-6L]:
    Pin 1 (I/O1) ── USB-C D–   (connector side)
    Pin 2 (GND)  ── GND
    Pin 3 (I/O2) ── USB-C D+   (connector side)
    Pin 4 (VBUS) ── USB-C VBUS  (5V high-side clamp reference)
    Pin 5 (I/O2) ── ESP32 IO20  (D+ MCU side)
    Pin 6 (I/O1) ── ESP32 IO19  (D– MCU side)
    Both I/O1 pins (1 & 6) are the two ends of the D– protection network.
    Both I/O2 pins (3 & 5) are the two ends of the D+ protection network.
    TVS diodes clamp each line to GND (low) and VBUS (high) during ESD events.
    Capacitance: 3.5pF max per line to GND (compliant with USB 2.0 full-speed).

 3. CAN BUS  (U3: SN65HVD230DR  +  J1: SM04B-GHS-TB JST GH 4-pin)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  SN65HVD230DR SOIC-8:

                    ┌──────────────────┐
  ESP32 IO16 TX ──►│ D   (pin 1)      │
             GND ───│ GND (pin 2)      │ CANH (pin 7) ──────┬───────────► J1 Pin 2
           3.3V ────│ VCC (pin 3)      │ CANL (pin 6) ──────┼─┬─────────► J1 Pin 3
  ESP32 IO17 RX ◄──│ R   (pin 4)      │                     │ │
                    │ Vref(pin 5) ─────────────────────── TP_VREF (1.65V = VCC/2)
             GND ───│ RS  (pin 8) ─────┘   ← tied to GND → high-speed mode (1 Mbps)
                    └──────────────────┘   └── TP_CANH     └── TP_CANL
                    └──────────────────┘

  ⚠ C_CAN: 100nF 0402 from VCC to GND — NOT IN SCHEMATIC (needs to be added)

  DroneCAN JST GH 4-pin — DUAL PORT (J1 and J1B), both wired to identical nets:
  (Allows daisy-chain bus topology without Y-splitter cables)

    J1  (Port 1 — e.g. cable IN from upstream node):
      Pin 1: +5V  ──┐
      Pin 2: CANH ──┼─── shared CAN_H net ──► SN65HVD230 CANH
      Pin 3: CANL ──┼─── shared CAN_L net ──► SN65HVD230 CANL
      Pin 4: GND  ──┤

    J1B (Port 2 — e.g. cable OUT to downstream node):
      Pin 1: +5V  ──┤
      Pin 2: CANH ──┤── same CAN_H net
      Pin 3: CANL ──┤── same CAN_L net
      Pin 4: GND  ──┘

    Shared +5V rail:
      Both J1/J1B Pin 1 ──[D3: SS54]──► 5V_BUS  ← accepts power from CAN bus
      (board can also supply 5V onto bus when XT30 is active — see power OR-ing)

  CAN Bus Termination (optional, end-of-line only):
    CANH ──[R9: 120Ω]──[CAN_TERM1: solder jumper, default OPEN]── CANL
    Close CAN_TERM1 only when this board is at the physical end of the CAN cable run.
    A properly terminated DroneCAN bus has exactly 120Ω at each physical cable end.

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
 5. ANALOG INPUT CHANNELS  ×3  (J4, J5, J6 — 3-pin 0.1" / 2.54mm headers)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Purpose: connect sensors or potentiometers outputting 0–5V.
  Voltage divider scales signal to 0–3.33V for ESP32 ADC (max 3.3V).
  Solder jumper bypasses divider for direct 0–3.3V sensor use.

  Header pinout (each J4/J5/J6):
    Pin 1: 5V_BUS  ← sensor power (nominally 5V, see power note above)
    Pin 2: GND
    Pin 3: Signal  (analog voltage in)

  Circuit (shown for CH_AN1, identical for CH_AN2 and CH_AN3):

  Signal Pin ──┬──────────────[JP2: solder jumper, default OPEN]──────────────┐
               │                                                               │
               └──[R15: 10kΩ 1%]────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │
                                                                              ▼ ▼
                                                                    ESP32 IO1 (ADC1_CH0)
                                                                              │
                                                                   ⚠[R_BOT: 20kΩ 1%] — NOT IN SCHEMATIC
                                                                   ⚠[C_AA: 100nF — anti-alias] — NOT IN SCHEMATIC
                                                                              │
                                                                             GND

  With JP OPEN  (default): Vsig × 20k/(10k+20k) = Vsig × 0.667
                            5.0V in → 3.33V at ADC  ✓ (full 5V range usable)
  With JP CLOSED (bypass): Signal connects directly to ESP32 ADC.
                            Use ONLY with ≤3.3V signals. ESP32 absolute max = 3.6V.

  Channel mapping:
    J4 (CH_AN1) Signal ── JP2 / R15 / (R_BOT missing) ──► IO1  (ADC1_CH0)
    J5 (CH_AN2) Signal ── JP3 / R16 / (R_BOT missing) ──► IO2  (ADC1_CH1)
    J6 (CH_AN3) Signal ── JP4 / R17 / (R_BOT missing) ──► IO3  (ADC1_CH2)


════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
 6. PWM OUTPUT CHANNELS  ×3  (J7, J8, J9 — 3-pin 0.1" / 2.54mm headers)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Purpose: drive servos, ESCs, or other 3.3V-logic PWM loads.
  No voltage divider. ESP32 GPIO drives signal pin directly (3.3V logic).
  Series resistor (R_S) limits GPIO short-circuit current.

  Header pinout (each J7/J8/J9):
    Pin 1: 5V_BUS  ← actuator power
    Pin 2: GND
    Pin 3: Signal  (PWM output, 3.3V logic level)

  Circuit (shown for CH_PWM1, identical for CH_PWM2 and CH_PWM3):

    ESP32 IO4 ──[R12: 33Ω series]──► Signal Pin (J7 Pin 3)

  Channel mapping:
    J7 (CH_PWM1) ── R12 ──► IO4   (LEDC ch0 / MCPWM capable)
    J8 (CH_PWM2) ── R13 ──► IO5   (LEDC ch1 / MCPWM capable)
    J9 (CH_PWM3) ── R14 ──► IO6   (LEDC ch2 / MCPWM capable)

  NOTE: Output is 3.3V logic. Most modern ESCs and servos accept 3.3V PWM.
        If 5V PWM is required, add an external level shifter between the header
        and the load — not implemented on this board.

 7. QWIIC I2C CONNECTORS  (J11 — SM04B-SRSS-TB, JST SH 1.0mm 4-pin)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Two independent I2C buses — use when devices share an address or need
  different bus speeds.
  ⚠ Only one Qwiic connector (J11) is currently in the schematic. Second connector (J12 or J10) needs to be added.

  J11 — I2C0  (IO8 / IO9):
    Pin 1: GND
    Pin 2: 3.3V
    Pin 3: SDA ──[R18: 4.7kΩ pull-up to 3.3V]──► ESP32 IO8
    Pin 4: SCL ──[R19: 4.7kΩ pull-up to 3.3V]──► ESP32 IO9
    ⚠ C_QW: 100nF 0402 from 3.3V to GND — NOT IN SCHEMATIC (needs to be added)

  ⚠ J11B (I2C1) — NOT IN SCHEMATIC (needs to be added, IO10 / IO11):
    Pin 1: GND
    Pin 2: 3.3V
    Pin 3: SDA ──[R_SDA2: 4.7kΩ pull-up to 3.3V]──► ESP32 IO10
    Pin 4: SCL ──[R_SCL2: 4.7kΩ pull-up to 3.3V]──► ESP32 IO11
    ⚠ C_QW2: 100nF 0402 from 3.3V to GND — NOT IN SCHEMATIC

  Pull-up note: 4.7kΩ on each bus. If Qwiic devices also have pull-ups the
  combined parallel resistance will be lower — acceptable for most devices.
  For 3+ devices on one bus, cut pull-ups on the Qwiic modules and rely on
  the on-board resistors only.

 11. DESIGN NOTES & WARNINGS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  [1] ANTENNA KEEPOUT: ESP32-S3-WROOM-1 PCB antenna area requires a copper-free
      keepout zone on the carrier board. No fills, traces, or vias allowed in
      the antenna projection area (approximately 3.5mm × 18mm at module end).

  [2] IMU PLACEMENT: Place ISM6HG256X away from power inductor L1.
      Switching inductors generate AC magnetic fields that induce noise in
      gyroscope and accelerometer measurements. Recommend ≥ 10mm clearance.
      Ideal placement: geometric center of board for balanced vibration response.

  [3] ANALOG SOLDER JUMPERS (JP2–JP4): Default OPEN = divider active, 0–5V
      input range. CLOSE to bypass divider for 0–3.3V signals only.
      Silkscreen each jumper with the channel number and "5V MAX / 3.3V BYPASS".

  [4] CAN TERMINATION (CAN_TERM1): Only nodes at the physical ends of the CAN cable
      need 120Ω termination. Leave CAN_TERM1 OPEN if any other device on the bus
      provides termination, or if this board is not at a cable end.

  [5] REVERSE POLARITY PROTECTION: SS56 Schottky (D7) in series with XT30+
      provides basic reverse polarity protection. Vf ≈ 0.4–0.5V. Combined with
      SMBJ58A TVS (D_TVS1), input is protected from reverse voltage and transient spikes.
      For absolute protection at all voltages, consider a P-FET + zener clamp.

  [6] 5V_BUS RAIL ACCURACY: When powered from XT30 via TPS54360B-Q1 (Vout ≈
      5.43V), 5V_BUS ≈ 4.9V after D5 Schottky drop (~0.55V Vf). When powered
      from USB-C or CAN bus (5.0V nominal), 5V_BUS ≈ 4.45V similarly.
      Analog and PWM headers will supply whichever is present. Most servos
      and sensors tolerate 4.5–5.5V. UVLO (R1/R2) prevents buck
      startup below ~8.5V VIN, protecting against brownout on low batteries.

  [7] PWM OUTPUT LEVELS: Signal pins on J7–J9 are 3.3V logic. Most ESCs (SBUS,
      standard PWM) and modern servos accept 3.3V. If a load strictly requires
      5V PWM logic, add an external level shifter (not on this board).

  [8] IMU CS BOOT STATE: R_CS (10kΩ pull-up to 3.3V on IO21) ensures CS is
      HIGH during ESP32 boot strapping sequence, preventing spurious SPI transactions
      while IO21 is in high-impedance input mode. ⚠ R_CS is NOT IN SCHEMATIC — needs to be added.

  [9] QWIIC PULL-UPS: R18 and R19 (4.7kΩ each) provide bus pull-ups for I2C0.
      If connecting multiple Qwiic devices each with their own 4.7kΩ pull-ups,
      the effective pull-up resistance lowers. For 3+ devices on the bus, consider
      cutting/removing pull-up jumpers on the Qwiic devices and relying on these
      onboard resistors only.

  [10] TOTAL ESTIMATED CURRENT (3.3V rail, nominal operation):
       ESP32-S3 active (WiFi TX): ~350 mA peak, ~150 mA average
       ISM6HG256X: ~0.8 mA
       SN65HVD230: ~5 mA
       LEDs: ~3 mA
       Qwiic device (typical): ~10 mA (varies by device)
       ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
       Total 3.3V peak: ~370 mA  (AP2112K rated 600mA — adequate for burst WiFi duty cycles)
